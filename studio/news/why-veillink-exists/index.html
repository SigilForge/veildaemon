Why VeilLink Exists

July 23, 2026 · Platform · Identity

← Studio News

At first glance, VeilLink appears to be an account system.

It is not.

VeilLink exists because the Archive needs to remember what belongs to whom.

Once someone acquires a recovery, that ownership becomes part of their Archive identity. Future revisions, updated editions, companion materials, audio releases, dossiers, and other recoveries can all be attached to the same operator without relying on payment emails or manual reconciliation.

Today that means Book One.

Tomorrow it may include:

  • Future novels
  • Needlepoints
  • Handler Guides
  • Audio books
  • Digital dossiers
  • Printable field materials
  • Special Archive recoveries
  • Print ownership verification

VeilLink is the connective tissue between every part of the Cradlepoint ecosystem.

It links the Archive to the Operator.
