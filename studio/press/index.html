Long

SigilForge Studios is an independent creative technology studio built around original transmedia horror-fantasy IP.

The studio develops tabletop publishing (Operator and Handler products, free and paid Needlepoint case modules), live Operator and Handler session software, supported by VeilLink’s account, ownership, and multi-device Table Live-Link infrastructure; session state moves through deliberate Cell synchronization rather than continuous background transfer. Book One and digital shelf assets, fiction, marketing and archive media, and a longer-horizon Mobile AR / Ritual Sites direction complete the public line.

SigilForge Studios’s commercial thesis is that tabletop, fiction, software identity, and physical-world participation are usually sold as disconnected products. SigilForge Studios connects them through one persistent emotional-physics system so each surface can reinforce the others.

J. Donavon Love is the systems designer, creative director, and canon authority. AI assists drafting and retrieval inside a governed pipeline; human release approval retains authority.

Documented internal production evidence (public figures rounded, with periods disclosed separately) includes 350K+ core rules words, 25 current final release PDFs, 130+ public wiki articles, live VeilDaemon infrastructure with an automated browser test suite, nearly 1K marketing images, and $0 paid acquisition through the July 10, 2026 snapshot.

Long bio 
J. Donavon Love is the founder of SigilForge Studios. He is the systems designer, creative director, and canon authority for the Cradlepoint universe and its product line.

His work began as a systems-design problem: how to describe invisible internal experience—pressure, memory, identity, connection—consistently enough that another person can reason about it. That model became emotional physics, then fiction, then playable tabletop procedure, then VeilDaemon software, with Mobile AR as a longer-horizon interface into physical space.

As a solo founder he has already produced IP, multi-book rules systems, shipping free and paid tabletop products, live Operator/Handler software, a public archive, commercial storefront materials, and a governed production pipeline with human release authority.
