About

Translation before mythology.

Cradlepoint began as a systems-design problem: make invisible internal experience legible enough to reason about together. Fiction, tabletop, software, and longer-horizon location work are interfaces to that model—not disconnected product experiments.

J. Donavon Love — systems designer, creative director, and canon authority.

How the work unfolded

A short timeline

  1. 01
    Support-system question

    How do you describe pressure, memory, identity, and connection consistently enough that another person can reason about them?

  2. 02
    Emotional physics

    A shared framework—not a diagnosis tool—for internal states that language usually fails under stress.

  3. 03
    Fiction as interface

    Story demanded answers the model had to make honest; the universe became a laboratory for the system.

  4. 04
    Tabletop procedure

    Play made the framework social: Operators, Handlers, Needlepoints, and shared consequence.

  5. 05
    VeilDaemon software

    Identity, records, and Handler tools so the table and archive can keep continuity between sessions.

  6. 06
    Toward place

    Mobile AR and Ritual Sites remain longer-horizon interfaces into physical space—scoped, not claimed as shipping SKUs.

How the studio operates

Human authority, assisted speed

AI assists drafting and retrieval inside a governed pipeline. Separate review passes and human release approval keep canon and public claims honest. Capital is meant to buy velocity and specialist finish—not permission to invent the first version of the work.
